Introduction

Bacteria Description

The bacteria Treponema pallidum pallidum was discovered as a causative agent of syphilis in 1905 by the german zoologist Frizt Schaudinn [2]. T. pallidum is a spirochete extremely fragile ex-vivo. Because of this the culture or genetic manipulation is very challenging, in laboratory this bacteria is cultivated in epithelial cells of rabbits to maintain the strains [3].

In spite of the difficulty of work with T. pallidum much has been learned about this pathogen.

About syphilis

Syphilis is one of the oldest known sexually transmitted infections. Despite all the prevention campaigns, the different types of treatment and ways to avoid it, the manifestation of this disease still growing in many parts of the world. The disease has four different stages described on Table 1.

Treatment

Since it was discovered the effect of penicillin on syphilis, the disease has been treated with it. For instance the penicillin G is frequently applied as treatment in patients in early stage of the disease [4]. In spite of the systematic use of this antibiotic on Treponema pallidum pallidum, the bacteria doesn’t demonstrate any resistance to the antibiotic [4].

With the increase of syphilis, particularly in China, Europe and the United States, even with an highly effective treatment, there has been a great demand on the part of scientists to create a vaccine to prevent the disease and the transmission [3].

Despite of the several studies already done, it isn’t still possible to produce an effective vaccine for syphilis [3]. So with a more profound analysis of each protein from this fragment, we tried to find new possible targets for further studies on vaccines or any other treatment approach.

Class Statistics



Graphic 1 - Statistic graphic with the absolute frequencies of the classes

This chart allows us to verify in a schematically way  how well is described the genome of the bacterium Treponema pallidum pallidum. We have found that in a total of 111 there are 73 proteins already described although, in many cases, their function has been identified by homology with other species.

However there are 38 proteins (proximally 1/3) called "hypothetical”. Once there’s not yet a known function, only an aminoacidic sequence indicative of a protein that can be produced.

Conclusion

﻿

Through the project the big gap for development available on this specie became evident.

In the literature, there have been described some known treatments, but no vaccines commercially available.

With protein by protein analysis, it were found two possible targets for this kind of approach: number 7 and 53, better described on the individual reports.

The bioinformatic approach allowed to predict the organism behavior, saving time and resources.

The reduced number of enzymes and proteins with metabolic (most of them are just products) or transport functions enabled to build metabolic and regulatory pathways or networks.

The molecular and biological processes obtained by the Gene Ontology(GO) analysis sustained the different classes. Further studies are required to improve the annotation scores (1 or 2 in the major part of the observed proteins), due to the the big number ‘unknown function’ proteins.

There’s a long tread to go, but Informatics connected with Biology can help overcome most of the obstacles on the way.
